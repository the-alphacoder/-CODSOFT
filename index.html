Tribute to Subash Chandra Bose

Introduction

Subash Chandra Bose was a prominent Indian nationalist whose defiant patriotism made him a hero in India. He is best known for his role in India's struggle for independence against British rule. Bose was a charismatic leader and is remembered for his advocacy for armed resistance and his efforts to secure freedom for India.

Early Life

Subash Chandra Bose was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, to Janakinath Bose and Prabhavati Devi. He was a brilliant student and completed his early education in Cuttack before moving to Calcutta (now Kolkata) for higher studies. Bose was deeply influenced by Swami Vivekananda's teachings and developed a strong sense of nationalism from an early age.

Education

Bose attended the Presidency College in Calcutta but was expelled due to his involvement in nationalist activities. He later completed his BA in Philosophy from the Scottish Church College. Bose went to England in 1919 to prepare for the Indian Civil Services (ICS) examination and was selected, but he resigned in 1921, citing his commitment to the freedom struggle.

Political Career

Subash Chandra Bose joined the Indian National Congress and rose to become the President of the Congress in 1938 and 1939. His radical views led to conflicts with other Congress leaders, particularly Mahatma Gandhi. In 1939, he formed the Forward Bloc within the Congress, advocating for complete and immediate independence from British rule.

Indian National Army

During World War II, Bose sought help from Axis powers to fight against the British. He traveled to Germany and then to Japan, where he took command of the Indian National Army (INA). The INA fought alongside Japanese forces against the British in Burma and India. Bose's leadership and his slogan, "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom," inspired many Indians to join the freedom struggle.

Personal Life

Subash Chandra Bose married Emilie Schenkl, an Austrian, in 1937. They had a daughter named Anita Bose Pfaff. Due to his political activities, Bose spent long periods away from his family, but his commitment to India's freedom remained unwavering.

Legacy

Subash Chandra Bose's legacy is one of courage, determination, and unwavering commitment to India's freedom. Despite controversies surrounding his alliances during World War II, his dedication to the cause of independence has made him a revered figure in Indian history. The story of the INA and Bose's efforts continue to inspire generations of Indians. His legacy is celebrated annually on his birthday, known as Subash Chandra Bose Jayanti, on January 23rd.
